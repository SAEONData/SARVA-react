• eventContribute Data and Media
    SARVA 3.0 offers a variety of tools and methods whereby officials and the public can contribute data and support information. This includes links to the National Climate Change Response Database (NCCRD), links to the Declared Disasters database maintained by NDMC, and a facility to supplement events and impacts with media uploaded from phones. Data can also be contributed via our links to SASDI (South African Spatial Data Infrastructure).
  • lightbulb_outlineNational Climate Change Response Database
    The National Climate Change Response Dataabase, hosted by DEA, provides officials with the opportunity to record mitigation and adaptation projects in their administrative area. This information, combined with options for mitigation and adaptation, allows assessment of future plans. Feasible options include projects identified in the BioEnergy Atlas, while options identified by international donors and the national government are also listed. We reflect Working for Water projects, as well as licenses gramted by NERSA for renewable energy.
  • warningEvents and Disasters
    SARVA 3.0 offers increased support for short-term events and disasters by way of a composite set of services. We blend data from the NDMC Declared Disasters database, satellite records of major hazards such as Fire, Drought, and Flood, news items, and social media streams mentioning any one of a number of hazards and disasters.
  • languageSASDI
    SARVA 3.0 is tightly integrated with SASDI. Metadata contributed to SASDI can be leveraged for inclusion into Profiling Tools and Atlases (with certain requirements in respect of the underlying datasets), and data contributed to SARVA can be marked for inclusion into SASDI. Either way, data will be mutually useful.

    Through SASDI, links are also maintained with the National Spatial Planning Data Repository (NSPDR)
